Heating

The radiators are controlled by smart thermostats which we manage remotely — if you're too warm or too cold, just message us and we'll adjust things straight away.
You can also adjust individual radiators yourself by twisting the head — turn towards + to increase the temperature or − to decrease it.
There is a handheld room thermostat in the lounge which can also be used to change the temperature in that room.
Please do not adjust the boiler settings or menu. The boiler is in the kitchen to the right of the kitchen window. If there's any issue with it, contact us and we'll sort it remotely or send someone out.

Hot water & shower

Hot water is available from the hot tap throughout the cottage.
The left switch outside the bathroom controls the extractor fan — please use it during and after showers.

Electricity

The fuse box is in the cupboard beside the front door. In the event of a power cut, there is an emergency torch in the drawer under the TV.
For power cut updates, visit enwl.co.uk.
Please do not touch the fuse box, boiler settings, or any gas fittings. If something isn't working, message us and we'll get it sorted.

Kitchen

Oven — Bosch electric single oven with fan and grill. Pre-heat for 10–15 minutes. Fan setting for an even bake; grill for browning and toasting.
Hob — Gas. Hold the igniter for 2–3 seconds after clicking until the flame catches. Wait a moment and try again if it doesn't light first time.
Coffee machine — Dolce Gusto. A couple of pods are provided to get you started — if you'd like more, these are widely available in supermarkets. Instant coffee sachets are also provided, and there is a cafetière if you prefer to use your own ground coffee.
Fridge & freezer — small fridge/freezer in the kitchen. There is a large chest freezer in the utility room outside.
Tea, coffee sachets & sugar are provided to get you started. The kitchen is otherwise fully equipped for self-catering.
Instructions for kitchen appliances are in the cupboard above the air fryer if needed.
Please use the extractor fan whenever the hob or oven is in use.
Spare bin liners are in the cupboard below the sink.
Please wash up and leave the kitchen as you found it. There is no dishwasher — washing-up liquid and a brush are at the sink.
Septic tank — important: No bleach or bleach-based products anywhere on site — not down the sink, not in the toilet, not for cleaning. Septic-tank-safe products only throughout.

Living room

TV — Panasonic remote. To change input, press Source and select HDMI. All remotes are on the coffee table.
Streaming — Roku stick is connected. Free Watch UK works without a login. Netflix, Prime Video, BBC iPlayer, Disney+, ITVX, and Channel 4 all require your own account — log in and out freely.

Log burner

Use 4 pieces of kindling and 2 logs to start a fire. Only burn wood — never plastic, treated timber, or any other materials.
Open both bottom air vents and the top air vent fully for the first 5 minutes to allow airflow.
Once established, spin all vents to near-closed to slow the burn.
There is a temperature gauge on top of the stove and a convection fan above — the fan starts automatically once the stove is hot enough.
Hot surfaces: Do not touch the sweep handle or door directly — both get extremely hot. Use the door handle turned anti-clockwise to open and close. Never leave the fire unattended with the door open.

Parking & utility room

Parking — gravel area in front of the large shed. Up to 4 cars.
The utility room is through the left door of the large shed. It contains the washing machine, tumble dryer, outdoor toilet, large chest freezer, and hose.
Washing machine — laundry pods are provided. Use one pod per load. 40° cotton is ideal for most clothes.
Tumble dryer — please wipe the lint filter clear after each use to prevent a fire risk.
A washing line and pegs are available in the garden if the weather allows.
Towels for drying pets are hung up in the utility room — please use these rather than the guest towels inside.

Outdoor WC & hose

There is an outdoor toilet in the utility room — perfect for returning from muddy walks so you don't trail anything through the cottage.
A hose is outside for rinsing muddy boots, dogs, and kit. Please coil it back up after use.

Garden & grounds

The cottage grounds extend to the gates — you are welcome to walk up to the gate by the chicken coops at the top of the site, but please do not go through any gate into the fields beyond.
There is barbed wire fencing along the boundary — please take care, especially with children and dogs.
BBQ — available for your use. Please clean and cover after use.
Outdoor lighting — exterior lights switch is by the main entrance and in the living room. Take care on stone steps and patio areas after dark — surfaces can be slippery when wet.
A note on the grounds: We do our best to keep the site clear, but as a rural property the wind can occasionally bring in debris and rabbit holes can appear in the grass. Please watch your step — particularly on wet or mossy stone — and keep a close eye on children and dogs when exploring the grounds.

Sheep & local wildlife

On rare occasions a sheep may find its way onto the site. If this happens, please let us know and we'll contact the local farmer — it won't affect your stay.
The sheep may be in lamb. Please keep dogs calm and at a distance, and do not let them approach or chase the sheep under any circumstances.

Gates

Please always close and latch the gates behind you — this keeps your pets safe within the grounds and ensures the property stays private and secure. An open gate is an invitation for animals to wander in or out.

General rules

No smoking indoors. Smoking is permitted outside — please dispose of cigarettes responsibly and do not drop litter.
No candles inside the cottage at any time.
Quiet hours — 10pm to 6am. Please be considerate of neighbours.
No parties or events without prior written consent.

Pets policy

Pets are welcome by prior arrangement — please declare them at the time of booking. Undeclared pets are not permitted.
Maximum 3 pets. Pets must be house-trained, flea-free, and in good health.
Pets are not permitted on beds or furniture at any time.
Pets must not be left unattended in the property unless crated.
Please clean up after your pets inside and outside. Do not use towels, bedding, or soft furnishings for pets.
Any excessive hair, odour, or damage may result in an additional cleaning or repair charge.
Please ensure pets do not disturb neighbours or wildlife — especially the sheep and lambs nearby.

Emergency procedures

Fire — evacuate immediately via the conservatory or main entrance. Close doors behind you. Meet at the parking area. Call 999.
Power cut — turn off the oven, microwave, and any other high-draw appliances. Use the torch from the TV drawer. Check enwl.co.uk for outage updates.
Water leak — the stopcock is under the kitchen sink. The water main is at the end of the driveway. Contact us immediately.
Please do not touch the gas or electric shutoffs unless directed by emergency services. If you suspect a gas leak — evacuate immediately, do not operate any switches, and call 999 from outside.
